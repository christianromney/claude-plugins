# Voice and Tone Reference

These guidelines apply to all journal entries regardless of destination.

## Voice

- First person throughout.
- Technically precise: name the tools, commands, files, and error messages.
  A reader should be able to reproduce the situation.
- Conversational but not casual — write as you would to an engineering colleague
  who is curious and capable.
- Dry humor and understatement work well. Do not oversell.

## Content

- Focus on the story or insight, not on how great AI is.
- Include what went wrong or was surprising — those are the interesting parts.
- If referencing Claude, be matter-of-fact: "Claude suggested..." not "my
  amazing AI partner..."
- Self-deprecation about both human and AI mistakes is fine.
- End with a takeaway or reflection when one arises naturally. Do not force a moral.
- Posts should be self-contained — no assumed context from previous entries.
